# Demo Journal Entries for Testing

## 📝 Entry 1: Reflective & Emotional (Good for testing "Why do I feel..." questions)

```
Today was a challenging day. I've been feeling overwhelmed with work lately, and it's starting to affect my sleep. I noticed I've been staying up until 2 AM trying to finish projects, and then I'm exhausted the next morning. 

I had a conversation with my manager today about my workload, and she was understanding. She suggested I take some time off next week to recharge. I think that's a good idea, but I'm also worried about falling behind.

On a positive note, I went for a walk in the park during lunch and it really helped clear my mind. The fresh air and sunlight made me feel more grounded. I should do that more often.

I'm realizing that I need to set better boundaries between work and personal time. It's hard because I care about doing a good job, but I also need to take care of myself.
```

**Mood**: 😔 (sad/stressed)

## 📝 Entry 2: Productive & Positive (Good for testing "When am I most productive?")

```
Had an amazing day today! Woke up early at 6 AM feeling refreshed and decided to tackle my most important project first thing. I was incredibly focused and productive - finished three major tasks before noon.

I've noticed that I'm most productive in the morning hours, especially between 7-10 AM. My mind is clear, I have more energy, and I can really dive deep into complex problems. I should structure my schedule to do the hardest work during these hours.

After lunch, I had a great meeting with the team where we brainstormed new ideas. I felt creative and engaged. The afternoon was a bit slower, but I still managed to complete a few smaller tasks.

Ended the day feeling accomplished and satisfied. Going to try to maintain this early morning routine.
```

**Mood**: 😄 (excited/happy)

## 📝 Entry 3: Anxious & Self-Reflective (Good for testing pattern detection)

```
Feeling anxious today. I have a big presentation coming up next week and I can't stop thinking about it. I've been preparing, but I keep worrying that I'll forget something important or that I won't be able to answer questions.

I've noticed this pattern before - whenever I have something important coming up, I get anxious about a week beforehand. My sleep gets worse, I have trouble focusing, and I second-guess myself constantly.

I tried some breathing exercises today which helped a bit. I also talked to a friend who reminded me that I always do well in these situations, even when I'm nervous. That helped put things in perspective.

I think the anxiety is partly because I care so much about doing well. But I need to remember that perfection isn't the goal - doing my best is enough.
```

**Mood**: 😔 (anxious)

---

## 📝 Entry 4: Grateful & Reflective (Good for testing mood patterns)

```
Today I'm feeling grateful. I spent time with my family this weekend and it reminded me of what's truly important. We had a simple dinner together, talked about our week, and just enjoyed each other's company.

I've been so focused on work and goals lately that I sometimes forget to appreciate the simple moments. These quiet family times are what I'll remember, not the stressful work days.

I'm also grateful for my health. I've been exercising regularly this month and I can feel the difference - more energy, better mood, clearer thinking. It's amazing how much physical activity affects my mental state.

Going to try to maintain this perspective and not let work stress overshadow the good things in my life.
```

**Mood**: 😊 (happy/grateful)

## 📝 Entry 5: Burnout & Reflection (Good for testing "Why was I burned out?")

```
I'm feeling completely burned out. This has been building for weeks. I've been working 12-hour days, skipping meals, and canceling plans with friends. I know this isn't sustainable, but I can't seem to stop.

Looking back at the past month, I can see the warning signs were there. I stopped exercising, my sleep got worse, and I started feeling irritable all the time. I should have recognized this earlier.

I had a moment of clarity today when a colleague asked how I was doing and I almost started crying. That's when I realized I need to make some changes. I can't keep going like this.

I'm going to talk to my manager tomorrow about reducing my workload. I need to prioritize my health and well-being. Work is important, but it's not worth sacrificing everything else.
```

**Mood**: 😔 (burned out)

## 🎯 Testing Scenarios

### After pasting these entries, try asking:

1. **"Why was I feeling burned out?"**
   - Should reference Entry 5 and patterns from other entries

2. **"When am I most productive?"**
   - Should reference Entry 2 about morning productivity

3. **"What patterns do you notice in my emotions?"**
   - Should identify anxiety patterns, work stress, etc.

4. **"How was my mood different in January vs now?"**
   - Temporal comparison (if you have entries from different times)

5. **"What should I do about my work stress?"**
   - Should give advice based on your journal evidence

## 💡 Tips

1. **Paste entries with different dates**: Use the date picker to create entries for different days
2. **Vary the moods**: Use different emoji moods to test mood filtering
3. **Wait for embeddings**: After saving, wait a few seconds for embeddings to generate
4. **Ask specific questions**: The more specific your question, the better the answer
